Frequently Asked Questions

ADOPTION & SURRENDERING:

How do I adopt a Dog from Puppy Path?

Answer: After choosing from our selection of very lovely dogs, rescued or otherwise surrendered, applicants are required to fill-up our adoption form and attend a scheduled screening Interview via Zoom that will determine your credibility to adopt one of our sweet dogs. Once we have approved of your case, 2-3 visits to our shelter to meet and get to know your chosen furry partner are required before you can bring them home.

Can my Adoption Application be denied?

Answer: Yes. One’s application form can be denied during any step of the process due to various reasons, such as being unable to keep their dog indoors, incompatibility with the household, and other factors and cases that that may cause damage to our dog’s health, safety, and happiness.

Can I Return my Adopted Pet if I change my Mind?

Answer: Owning a pet itself is a lifetime commitment and should not be taken lightly with little thought. However if you really cannot keep your pet, do not just abandon them and instead just return them to our shelter.

Do you have any Purebred Dogs?

Answer: It is very rare that purebred dogs are admitted to the shelter. Sadly, they are valued more than aspins who are equally deserving of a home. Please consider adopting a local breed instead.

I live in the Province/Abroad, Can I still Adopt?

Answer: Yes, but special arrangements need to be made for the meet-and-greet, depending on your location. Please contact us to discuss your options. You may also opt to adopt from your local pound instead.

How do I surrender my dog for Relocaton?

Answer: Home relocation is a more delicate process and will require a much more meticulous consideration. After the applicants have both filled-out the owner’s and pet’s application, a scheduled face-to-face screening will be held at our main office, as well as a conducting a thorough background check and scheduled pet check-up which you will present to us. In the event that the applicant has passed all requirements, only then will their canine be handed over to our care for home relocation(adoption).
